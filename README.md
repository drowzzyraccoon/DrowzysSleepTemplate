HOW TO USE:


ü¶ù How to Customize the Drowzy Ombre Template
Welcome to the Ombre Glass template! This theme is designed to be super easy to customize, even if you don't know much about coding.

You do not need to scroll through hundreds of lines of code to change how this looks. Everything you need is right at the very top of the CSS file in Section 1: The Control Panel.

Here is a quick guide on how to tweak the theme to match your style:

1. Changing the Colors
Find the /* ---- A) THEME COLORS ---- */ section.

--COLOR-1, 2, and 3: These control the moving ombre gradient at the top of your panels.

Colors are written in rgba(red, green, blue, transparency) format. To find new RGB codes, you can just search "Color Picker" on Google! The last number (like 0.70) is the transparency. 1 is solid, and 0 is completely invisible.

2. Adjusting the "Glass" Look
Want your panels to look more transparent or more frosted?

Change the --CREAM-TOP and --CREAM-BOTTOM variables. Lowering the last number in the brackets makes the glass more see-through.

Find the --BLUR variable. Change 10px to a higher number (like 20px) for a super frosted look, or lower it to 0px if you don't want any background blur at all.

3. Controlling the Neon Glow & Shadows
Under /* ---- C) GLOW & HOVER EFFECTS ---- */:

Turn it off: If you don't like the glowing effect, change --GLOW-ON from 1 to 0.

Make it stronger: Increase --GLOW-STRENGTH (e.g., to 40px) to make the panels radiate more light.

Change the shadow colors: Under Section D, update --SHADOW-COLOR-1 and --SHADOW-COLOR-2 to change the color of the deep shadows floating behind your panels.

4. Changing the Roundness
If you prefer sharp corners over bubbly ones, look at /* ---- B) ROUNDNESS + SPACING ---- */. Change --R-LG (Large), --R-MD (Medium), and --R-SM (Small) to 0px for perfectly square boxes, or make the numbers bigger for pillowy, rounded edges.
